Key Definitions

Dormant courier
Active (≥1 delivery) in weeks 5–24 ago, zero deliveries in the last 4 weeks. Primary CRM reactivation target.
RTD conversion
Couriers who made their first delivery this week (Ready-To-Deliver). Proxy for newly activated supply.
WoW %
Week-over-week change in active couriers. Negative values indicate supply contraction.
Min. city size
Only cities with ≥ 100 active couriers are included — smaller cities have too little data to score reliably.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Reactivation Opportunity Engine? +
A weekly automated system that ranks Wolt cities by how much untapped courier supply is available through reactivation. Each Monday it pulls last week's data from Snowflake, computes a weighted priority score per city, and updates this dashboard. The goal is to help CRM and Operations teams direct their reactivation campaigns to the cities where they'll have the most impact.
How is the Priority Score calculated? +
Each city gets four component scores (0–100), each normalised against all cities. The composite is: Score = 0.35 × Demand + 0.25 × Supply Decline + 0.25 × Dormant + 0.15 × ETA. Demand = orders per courier. Supply Decline = courier week-over-week drop (0 if positive). Dormant = count of couriers dormant 5–24 weeks. ETA = average delivery time. All values are normalised by dividing by the city-level maximum across all markets.
What is a dormant courier and why target them? +
A dormant courier made at least one delivery between 5 and 24 weeks ago but has had zero deliveries in the last 4 weeks. They are the highest-value reactivation target because they already know the platform — no onboarding friction — and their churn is often due to addressable reasons (low offer frequency, life events, competitor engagement). Reactivating one dormant courier costs far less than acquiring a new one.
Why do some high-order cities rank low? +
The score reflects reactivation opportunity, not absolute volume. A large city with a healthy, growing supply base and short ETAs scores low even with high order volumes, because there is little signal that reactivation is needed. Cities rank highly when demand is rising relative to supply, couriers are churning (negative WoW), a large dormant pool exists, and ETAs are stretched — all pointing to a supply gap that reactivation can close.
Where does the data come from? +
All data is sourced from Wolt's Snowflake instance (DOORDASH-IG78751_AWS_EU_WEST_1, PRODUCTION database). Supply and WoW data: PRODUCTION.COURIER.CITY_METRICS_GD. Order and ETA data: PRODUCTION.COURIER.FLEET_SUPPLY_HOURS. Dormant pool: PRODUCTION.COURIER.COURIER_PARTNER_METRICS. RTD and onboarding: PRODUCTION.COURIER.ONBOARDING_METRICS_GD.
When does the dashboard update? +
Every Monday at 08:00 (CET), the engine queries Snowflake for the previous week's data, scores all cities, and pushes a fresh data.json to this GitHub repository. The page loads that file directly — no authentication required. The data always reflects the most recently completed ISO week.
